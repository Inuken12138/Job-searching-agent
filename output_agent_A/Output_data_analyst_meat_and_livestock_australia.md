## JD TL;DR for Human User

- Main work: ensure smooth data flow from industry partners, deliver market intelligence solutions, develop and maintain dashboards and analytics for NLRS and Market Information reporting, and collaborate across MLA to improve business processes.

## Supplementary Notes for Agent B

- Work-rights note: the JD says, "The successful candidate must hold an appropriate visa/residency status for full-time employment in Australia." This is a general eligibility requirement, not an explicit citizenship or PR-only condition.
- Strongest selling point: the SongFei role gives direct evidence of reporting automation, process improvement, management-facing analytics, and business-oriented solution delivery.
- Strongest selling point: the Tableau dashboards, Excel/VBA reporting system, and PostgreSQL/database background create a credible tools story for dashboarding and SQL-adjacent analysis, while remaining honest about the lack of direct Power BI experience.
- Strongest selling point: the microtransit capstone and DCG Global work show stakeholder communication, requirements gathering, reporting, and collaboration with both business and technical teams.
- Nuance to preserve: do not claim direct Power BI, direct production AWS experience, or direct agriculture industry exposure. Frame Tableau, SQL/database studies, S3-level coursework exposure, and business-facing project work as adjacent foundations and fast-ramp-up evidence.
- Most critical rows to carry into the final letter: the JD-fit row, the competitive-edge row, and the first motivation row. Together they make the strongest case for a junior-to-early-career analyst who can improve reporting and work well across stakeholders.
- Narrative thread: position the candidate as a business-aware, technically grounded analyst-builder who enjoys turning messy operational data into useful reporting, clearer decisions, and better processes.
- Optional tone guidance: keep the final letter practical and grounded rather than overly polished. The strongest voice for this application is clear, thoughtful, collaborative, and honest about adjacent versus direct tool experience.
